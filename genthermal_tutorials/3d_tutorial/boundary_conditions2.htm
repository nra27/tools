Thermal Stream 1

Thermal streams are the most widely used of all the thermal boundary conditions and are used to define a portion of the boundary that has a finite flow of fluid along its length. 

Select Thermal Stream... from the Thermal Menu and complete the Thermal Stream panel as shown below:

The flow W1L001 is derived from SPAN.

More information is available about nfdw and fcd.

Point and click the model faces D and E with the left mouse button.

Full details of all windage, heat transfer coefficient and mass flow correlations are given in Best Practice for Empirical Heat Transfer & Windage Correlations.

Thermal Stream 4

Use the Last Command option to select Thermal - Streams

Complete the Thermal Stream panel as shown on the right

Then point and click on the model faces J & K with the left mouse button.

 

 

 

  • Boundary conditions are applied to a face enclosed by 'boundary lines'. Use the Display/Display Settings command to switch boundary lines on and note the boundary lines inside the cooling holes.

  • Notice that the mass flow in Thermal Stream 4 is shown to be w1L002/24. This is because w1L002 is the total mass flow for the 24 holes on the entire disc so a single hole's mass flow will be defined as w1L002/24 ( total mass flow / 24 = mass flow of 1 hole ).

  • More information is available about fcd.

 

Thermal Void

Thermal voids are used when a portion or portions of the boundary are in contact with a fluid that is deemed to have negligible thermal capacity.

Select Voids... from the Thermal Menu and complete the thermal Void panel as shown on the right

 

Then point and click the model face H with the left mouse button.

 

 

              

The power input term allows an additional heat input to the void. In this case 5% of stream 1 flow is fed into the void calculation.

Repeated Temperatures

The Repeated Temperatures option is used to define surfaces on which temperatures should be identical, e.g. the faces of cyclic sectors. This ensures that the cyclic faces are not assumed to be adiabatic surfaces, but will exchange heat with each other to ensure thermal symmetry.

Select Repeated Temps... from the Thermal Menu and then Sector from the Repeated Temperatures panel

 

Then point and click the model faces J & K with the left mouse button.

The Repeated Temperatures boundary conditions are automatically assigned to the other corresponding sector faces.

 

If this does not work, refer to the SC03 Errors Dictionary and search for the error to solve the problem, or see below.

 

  • If it is not possible to define the repeated temperature boundary conditions, follow the steps below:

 

  1. Select Delete and then Repeated Temperatures, and use right-hand-mouse to Delete All to make sure that all repeated temperature boundary conditions have been removed

  2. Delete the Mesh (i.e. Select Delete, and then Generate Mesh)

  3. Make sure that the Geometry is present from Display Settings, and then (while there is no mesh present) define the repeated temperature boundary conditions:

  1. Remesh the model (Mesh/Generate Mesh).  Note that the repeated temperatures boundary conditions will now force the sector faces to have identical mesh patterns.
Property Display Setting

Open the 'Display Menu' by clicking on Display and then choose Property Display from the menu.

Then select the Thermal tab from the five tab options.

Now you can select different colours to highlight the different boundary conditions that you have just applied.

First, select a colour from the palette

Then click on a Col button next to the boundary condition required. (You have just defined Conv. Zone, Duct, Void and Stream conditions).

Then click on OK to apply the different settings.
