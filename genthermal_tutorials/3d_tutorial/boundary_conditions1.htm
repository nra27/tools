SC03 3D Thermal Modelling

Section 4: Thermal Boundary Conditions

Objectives of this Section

When you have completed this section you will be able to:

  • define boundary conditions in SC03

  • define the appropriate heat transfer correlation for a particular situation

  • define the non-dimensional terms within heat transfer correlations

  • explain the applicability of convecting zones, thermal ducts, thermal streams and thermal voids in the definition of boundary conditions

Learning Points of this Section

There are four stages in boundary preparation:

  1. Boundary condition preparation

  2. Interpretation of the problem

  3. Representation of the problem using SC03 boundary conditions

  4. Definition of boundary conditions within SC03 using:

  • convecting zones

  • thermal streams

  • thermal voids

Boundary Condition Preparation

The preparation of boundary conditions is the most important aspect of creating a quality SC03 Model.

The accuracy with which the true engineering problem can be represented by boundary conditions will determine the validity of the analysis model.

The definition of boundary conditions can be broken down into the following stages.

Boundary Condition Definition Stages

  • Interpretation of engineering problem

  • Representation of problem using SC03 boundary conditions

  • Definition of boundary conditions within SC03

  • Checking of assigned boundary conditions

Each of these stages will now be completed for the simple problem under consideration.

Interpretation of the problem

A thorough review of the problem requiring analysis must be completed.

This will usually mean:

  • discussion with those people who have a detailed knowledge of the component, its design features, performance and/or service operation

  • reference to previous related or similar analyses and to validation experience.

The Problem

As explained in the User Guide, the problem to be tackled in this training module consists of a simple disc and shaft assembly. 

For the purposes of this course all boundary condition assumptions will be provided. 

The disc incorporates 24 cooling air holes at a radius of 260 mm, while an inner shaft inside the main shaft/disc assembly rotates in the same direction, but at a lower speed.

The problem is to determine the temperature and stress distributions in the rotating disc.

Representation of the problem

Once a thorough understanding of the problem has been achieved, the problem must be converted to an appropriate representation in SC03.

This requires:

  • awareness of the different boundary condition types, their requirements and limitations

  • reference to previous related or similar analyses and validation experience

  • a sketch of all boundary conditions to be assigned and their associated expressions.

 

The tutorial problem can be simplified by modelling a single 15 degree sector of the disc/shaft assembly and assuming the same temperature and stresses are repeated in all other sectors.

    

In this training module, all boundary conditions are provided.  However, full details of all Thermal Menu commands are provided in the SC03 Training Section: 'Learn about SC03 Commands'

  • If you want to learn more about the Thermal Menu commands now, go to the Thermals homepage to follow the Quick Link to the SC03 Users Guide (Section 6.3 Thermal Modelling)

  • The Thermal Modelling section also includes a full description of each Thermal boundary condition type, as well as the following guidance which can be used when representing a problem in SC03:

  • Guidance in applying Thermal Boundary Definitions

  • Guidance in calculating Thermal Boundary Conditions

 

Definition of Boundary Conditions

We will now define the boundary conditions for our problem in SC03.

If SC03 is not already running, start SC03 and open the database saved at the end of the previous section. 

Open the 'Thermal Menu' by selecting Thermal with the left mouse button

then Select the Convecting Zones command.

Convecting zone 1

Convecting zones are used to define convection to a known fluid temperature on a geometry face.

Complete the Convecting Zone panel as shown in the table, then select OK on the panel.

 

Did you know...?

  • The fluid type and pressure are used in most heat transfer coefficient correlations.

  • The expression
              t26 + 1.023*(t30-t26)
    is used to scale the temperature at this point in the model from the known engine temperatures t30 and t26.

  • The pressure p1c01 is derived from SPAN.

  • nh is the speed of the HP shaft in the engine. For more about frgend, follow this link.

  • Factors are applied to match measurements taken from engines.
