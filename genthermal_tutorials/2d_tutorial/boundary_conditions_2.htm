Convecting Zone 1

Convecting zones are used to define convection to a known fluid temperature on a geometry face. 

Complete the Convecting
Zone panel as shown in the table on the right, then select OK on the panel.

 

Did you know...?

  • The fluid type and pressure are used in most heat transfer coefficient correlations.

  • The expression
              t26 + 1.023*(t30-t26)
    is used to scale the temperature at this point in the model from the known engine temperatures t30 and t26.

  • The pressure p1c01 is derived from SPAN.

  • nh is the speed of the HP shaft in the engine. For more about frgend, follow this link.

  • Factors are applied to match measurements taken from engines.

 

For a listing of the HTCs available in SC03, follow this link.

 

SC03 is non case-specific and can therefore accept upper or lower case letters.

 

To define the Convecting Zone, point and click on points A and C (ignore point B).

 

If you make a mistake in applying any of the boundary conditions, then to delete them do the following: -

  • Click on the Set Delete button on the status bar at the bottom right hand corner of the screen.

  • Select Last command with the right mouse button.

  • Click on the incorrect boundary condition with the left mouse button.

Press the right mouse button in the SC03 display window and select Last Command.

Complete the Convecting Zone panel as shown below.

 

Then point and click on points D and E.
                                 

Thermal Duct

Thermal ducts are used to define two portions of the boundary between which there is advective flow of finite thermal capacity. 

Select Ducts... from the Thermal Menu and complete the Thermal Duct panel as shown on the right.

 

 

  • The flow W1L001 is derived from SPAN.

  • More information is available about nfdw and fcd.

 

Then point and click on points A and B (for the first side of the duct) and again on C and D (for the second side of the duct ): 

    • The thermal duct panel has a toggle option to allow the expressions for the first side of the duct to be repeated as the responses for the second side. The toggle can be turned off if each side requires a different response.

 

Thermal Stream 1

Thermal streams are the most widely used of all the thermal boundary conditions and are used to define a portion of the boundary that has a finite flow of fluid along its length. 

Select Thermal Stream... from the Thermal Menu and complete the Thermal Stream panel as shown below:

                

The message Doubtful use of environment parameter NH may be seen - ignore this.

  • More information is available about nvp.

 

Then point and click on points A and B:

Full details of all windage, heat transfer coefficient and mass flow correlations are given in the Best Practice for Empirical Heat Transfer & Windage Correlations
