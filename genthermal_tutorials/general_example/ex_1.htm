Exercise 1 - Disc Temperature Analysis

A problem often encountered in the detail design phase of a project is the optimisation of component geometry in particular disc profiles.

Exercise Objectives

To examine the effect of a change in disc cob width on the temperature distributions of the HPT disc.

Problem

Given the basic model described in the preceding sections, reduce the HP turbine disc cob width by 25% to 90mm and determine:

  • The change in response rates of the HPT disc rim and cob centre temperatures

  • The change in peak HPT disc rim to cob centre temperature difference

  • The change in temperature distribution at the time of peak rim to cob centre temperature difference

 

Instructions

Complete a thermal analysis of the basic SC03 model (general.example.start..pm) and plot graphs of the following:

  • Plot 1:  HPT disc rim and cob centre temperatures as a function of time over the square cycle

  • Plot 2:  HPT disc rim to cob centre temperature difference as a function of time

  • Plot 3:  Temperature contours (K) in the cob at the time of peak rim to cob centre temperature difference.

Click here to see ideal locations of reference points

Click here to find out about live and dead rims

 

Make a copy of the basic model (example.pm) and save it as exercise1.pm

Use sc03 to modify the HPT disc width.

Click here to see before & after geometry  

 

Complete a thermal analysis of the reduced width disc cob model and plot graphs of the following:

  • Plot 4:  HPT disc rim and cob centre temperature for the basic model and the reduced width cob design on the same plot

  • Plot 5:  HPT disc rim to cob centre temperature differences for both models on the same plot

  • Plot 6:  Temperature contours (K) in the cob at the time of peak rim to reduced cob centre temperature difference

Click here to get help creating graphs with plots of two different models on one plot.

 

Discuss the results with your training supervisor.
