Make Thermal Model Thermo-Mechanical

 

Use Global Controls to make run thermo-mechanical.

Use Domain Control/Structural Loads to make all the domains corresponding to the HPT Disc & Blade assembly to be 'structurally enabled' as shown below. For all other domains, deselect the 'enabled' toggle on the panel below.

 

Applying Structural Boundary Conditions

Apply prescribed displacement (definition shown below) to most forward active structural piece of geometry as shown below.

  1. Click on Structural/Prescribed Displacements to show the panel below.

 

  1. Click on Structural/Prescribed Displacements to show the panel below.

Leave the displacement and Active fields as 0. Use Cycle Definition/Output to ensure stress option is turned to output results.

Hoop Stress

Hoop Stress is circumferential stress, represented in sc03 as SigmaZZ (SZZ).

Max Hoop Stress is always at a maximum at the bore of a disc.

Do a time plot of SZZ at the disc bore, determine the maximum hoop stress, and plot the contours at this point.

 

Plot 1 2334.91s Ramp 4, Step 20
Plot 3 2338.69s Ramp 4, Step 20

 

Worst Principal Stress

Worst Principal Stress in the XY plane is represented in sc03 as SWXY

Do a time plot of temperature difference between the rim and the cob of the disc, determine the point of maximum temperature difference, and plot the contours at this point.

Plot 2 2152.51s Ramp 4, Step 17
Plot 4 2122.71s Ramp 4, Step 16

 

Interpolation Help

  1. Run the thermal model of the whole assembly as usual

  2. In the HPT disc model, either keep the thermal boundary conditions on, or apply pressure loads to account for the pressure loads.

  3. Interpolate the temperatures from the assembly model onto the HPT disc model

    • In the Model menu, select Interpolation Temps... to reveal the Temperature Interpolation input box.

    • Make Set = 1 - the sets the interpolated temperature set about to be read in to 1.

    • The Stress all Time Points toggle should be OFF as shown below

  1. On clicking OK, the temperatures will be interpolated read in from the .fem file specified.

  2. An information box will appear as shown below:

  1. As this will be a structural analysis, the interpolation temperatures should be specified in the Structural tab within Domain control as shown below.

  1. The model should now be run structurally in order to interpolate the temperatures onto the model. Note that it is not possible to display the interpolated temperatures without running the model.
