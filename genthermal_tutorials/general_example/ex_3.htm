Exercise 3 - Mass Flow Analysis

The amount of air diverted to cool the internal structure affects the performance of the engine. It is therefore necessary to optimise the amount of cooling air passing over a given component. This problem will examine the effect changing the mass flow has on the temperature of the HP compressor discs.

Exercise Objectives

To examine the effect of a change in compressor drum bore mass flow on the temperature distributions of the HP Compressor discs.

Problem

Given the basic model described in the preceding sections, increase the compressor drum bore flow by a factor of 2 and determine:

  • The HP compressor disc cob transient temperatures

  • The steady state max take-off (MTO) temperature changes.

Instructions

Using the results of the basic SC03 model (example.pm) plot:

  • Plot 1:  Graphs of HPC cob centre temperatures as a function of time over the square cycle

  • Plot 2:  Temperature contours (K) at the time of peak HPC cob centre temperatures
